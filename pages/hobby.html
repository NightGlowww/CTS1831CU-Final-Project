MY PASSION

On January 28, 2020, I acquired my first camera, the Sony A6600. The first photo I took with the camera was of the flowers at my uncle's house. Then I went outside and took some random pictures on the streets near my home. When I got home and reviewed the shots, I felt that the camera didn't produce the results I had idealized. The colors seemed average, except for being slightly clearer and more naturally blurred than those taken with a phone. There didn't seem to be anything particularly special about them. So, I started to search on Baidu why the photos taken with a camera felt quite ordinary. That's when I began learning about photography in a new field.

During this process, I watched numerous video tutorials and gradually understood the basics of exposure, color correction, composition, and other aspects of image configuration. I finally grasped some knowledge, but I still needed practical experience and to go out and take more photos. At that time, I felt so happy taking pictures, capturing anything that looked beautiful, and it felt quite satisfying. Gradually, I fell in love with that feeling, wanting to document beautiful things and moments. During this period, I photographed streets at sunset, the serene Nanjing Road, and the morning sunrise at the Bundâ€”meaningful photos that captured Shanghai during the pandemic.

After the situation of the pandemic improved relatively, I traveled to more places for photography. However, my photos ended up being similar to those taken by tourists. Beautiful sights would become boring after a while, and I wouldn't spend too much time dwelling on them. So, I started to contemplate the meaning of photography and how to capture better photos. I began thinking about how to enhance the artistic aspect of my photographs. I bought a book by Teacher Zhang Qianli titled "So That's How You Take Photos." After flipping through it, I felt like my mindset had somewhat improved, enabling me to use better composition to describe a photo. But even with this, the meaning of photography remained somewhat unclear to me.

It wasn't until after school started that I understood the significance of photography to me. One day after school started, I opened my photo album to find a group photo taken when we first started school. As I flipped through the album, I came across photos taken after our outdoor activities and our daily moments together. Those photos, although not high-resolution and lacking composition, stirred up beautiful memories of our early days in school and the little joys and antics of my classmates. These photos weren't high-quality in terms of resolution or composition, but they documented our growth. Comparing the group photo from a year ago to the one from a year later, it was truly a blissful experience.

That's when I realized that I loved photography, capturing the beauty of life. Even before I bought a camera, I used my phone to capture the beautiful moments in life. Getting a camera allowed me to preserve these moments with higher quality, enabling me to engage in better artistic creation. Photography taught me to cherish the people and things in front of me. That's the meaning of photography to me.

In this new year, I hope to better capture the beauty in front of me and discover the extraordinary in life. Let's create better works!~
